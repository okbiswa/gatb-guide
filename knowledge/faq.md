# FAQ & Mentor Advice

## 1. What score is safe for top institutes?
"A score is generally considered safe if it is 5 to 10 points above the historical cutoff for your category. However, cutoffs fluctuate yearly based on the exam difficulty and the number of candidates."

## 2. How are weighted cutoffs and margins calculated?
"The margin is simply your GAT-B score minus the historical cutoff for that institute. A positive margin means your score is higher than the cutoff (Safe/High-Match). A negative margin means your score is slightly below the cutoff (Ambitious)."

## 3. Which institute is best for Genetics?
"JNU and University of Delhi (North Campus) are premier institutes known for strong genetics programs. UoH also has an excellent molecular genetics department."

## 4. Which institute is best for Cancer Biology?
"Rajiv Gandhi Centre for Biotechnology (RGCB) in Kerala is a leading research institute that specializes heavily in cancer biology, disease biology, and biomedical research."

## 5. Should I prefer JNU or IIT Indore?
"Both are exceptional. Choose IIT Indore if you want an Integrated M.Sc.-Ph.D. with a strong focus on structural biology, biomaterials, or biomedical engineering. Choose JNU if you want a classic 2-year M.Sc. with a strong interdisciplinary life sciences curriculum."

## 6. What is the difference between MSc and MTech?
"M.Sc. Biotechnology is a science degree focusing on theory and research (molecular biology, microbiology, etc.), while M.Tech. is engineering-oriented (bioprocess engineering, devices). Choose M.Sc. for lab science interests; M.Tech if you prefer technical design and instrumentation."

## 7. What is an Integrated MS-PhD?
"An Integrated M.Sc.-Ph.D. is a 5-year program leading directly to a PhD. You finish your M.Sc. coursework in ~2 years, then proceed to PhD research without reapplying. It’s ideal if you’re certain about pursuing a research career."

## 8. Are there interviews for admission?
"Most DBT-supported M.Sc. programs base admission strictly on your GAT-B rank/score with no interviews. However, specific specialized programs or certain institutes (like RCB or IGIB) may admit solely by rank or use interviews for some cohorts."

## 9. What are the career options after MSc Biotechnology?
"Graduates often pursue higher studies (Ph.D.) at top institutes in India or abroad. Other options include R&D roles in pharmaceutical companies, bioinformatics, data analysis, clinical research, or scientific writing."

## 10. How should I shortlist colleges?
"Always create a balanced preference list. Include 'Dream/Ambitious' colleges (where your score is slightly below the cutoff), 'High-Match' colleges (where you comfortably meet the cutoff), and 'Safe/Backup' colleges (where you exceed the cutoff by a large margin). Also consider location and research interests."

## 11. Eligibility Criteria
"Typically, you need a 3-year B.Sc. (or relevant degree) with ≥55% overall (50% for SC/ST) to apply for DBT M.Sc. seats. Accepted majors usually include any basic sciences, biotech, pharmacy, engineering, or agriculture."
