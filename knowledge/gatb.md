# GAT-B (Graduate Aptitude Test - Biotechnology)

## What is GAT-B?
GAT-B is the Graduate Aptitude Test - Biotechnology, an all-India entrance examination conducted by the Regional Centre for Biotechnology (RCB) on behalf of the Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Government of India. It is the single entrance exam for admission to DBT-supported Post Graduate academic programs in Biotechnology and allied areas at participating institutions.

## Degree Programs Explained
- **M.Sc. Biotechnology**: A science degree focusing on theory and research (molecular biology, microbiology, genetics). Best for students with lab science interests.
- **M.Tech. Biotechnology**: An engineering-oriented degree (bioprocess engineering, devices, computational modeling). Best for technical design and instrumentation.
- **Integrated M.Sc.-Ph.D.**: A 5-year combined program leading directly to a PhD. Students finish their M.Sc. coursework in ~2 years, then proceed to PhD research without reapplying. Ideal for those certain about a research career.

## Reservation Rules
Government rules reserve seats as follows:
- SC/ST candidates receive relaxation (50% marks cutoff in qualifying degrees).
- OBC candidates receive category quotas and age relaxations.
- EWS (10%) and horizontal categories (PwD, etc.) apply as per individual institute norms.

## Cutoff Interpretations
- **Cutoff Score**: The GAT-B score of the last admitted candidate. 
- **Margin**: The difference between your score and the historical cutoff.
- **Safe / High-Match**: Institutes where your score is above the historical cutoff (by ≥5–10 points).
- **Ambitious**: Institutes where your score is slightly below the cutoff.
Always include both types in your application preferences to balance risk. Cutoffs fluctuate yearly based on exam difficulty and applicant volume.

## Counselling Timeline
- GAT-B results usually publish by June.
- DBT uploads a list of participating institutes by July.
- Counseling and seat allotment at individual universities often runs July–August.
- Students must keep checking DBT/GAT-B official notices and individual institute websites for exact dates.

## Eligibility Criteria
Typically, you need a 3-year B.Sc. (or relevant degree) with ≥55% overall (50% for SC/ST) to apply for DBT M.Sc. seats. Accepted undergraduate majors generally include any basic sciences, biotechnology, pharmacy, engineering, or agriculture.

## Interview vs Non-Interview Admissions
- Some institutes (like RCB or IGIB) admit solely by rank or have specialized programs with interviews.
- However, standard DBT-supported programs generally do NOT have interviews and base admission entirely on the GAT-B score.
- If an institute has an interview (e.g. RCB for specific programs), candidates should review B.Sc. projects, biotech fundamentals (genetics, cell biology), and be ready to discuss why they chose that college.
